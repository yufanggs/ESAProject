Design Rules

General Rules

1. Respect the autonomy of working members in their respective area of work.

2. Participate in weekly meeting to keep everyone on the same page.

3. Generate weekly report to keep track of work progress and updates.

 

Roles

Logic Designer: Check the consistency and requirements of the overall design. Designs and write instructions of the game based on the mission statement and needs of stakeholders.

Graphic Designer: Standardise the overall design of the product, and also video production.

Logistic Manager: In charge of purchasing materials and booking facilities to carry out production as well as keeping updates on the project expenses.

Artifact Designer: Design and manufacture game pieces, question cards, opportunity cards, dice, game board.

Package Designer: Design the package, for example, choose the shape of the packages, the arrangements of components inside the box to fit the components well.

 

Role Diagram: Working relationship between team members of the project

 

1. Logic Designer works hand in hand with the Graphic Designer. The Graphic Designer creates visually appealing aesthetics of the game (board, cards, etc) based on the game rules designed by the Logic Designer.

2. Graphic Designer provides rough sketches of game equipment for the Artifact Designer to start shaping raw materials into detailed aesthetic game pieces.

3. Graphical Artist provides a rough operation sketch for the Package Designer to work on and finalise the image to be displayed on the package.

4. Logistic Manager work with Artifact Designer and Package Designer to settle budget, materials purchases and facilities booking for the project.

5. Artifact Designer works hand in hand with the Package Designer to decide the sizes and arrangement of game components of pieces.
